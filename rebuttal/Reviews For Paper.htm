Question 
[Paper Summary] What is the paper about? Please, be concise (3 to 5 sentences) The paper proposes a new method for text-to-image synthesis. The main contribution is a new GAN with hierarchical discriminators that work at different levels of output resolution. The results are very good.
[Paper Strengths] Please discuss, justifying your comments with the appropriate level of details, the strengths of the paper (i.e. novelty, theoretical approach and/or technical correctness, adequate evaluation, clarity, etc). For instance, a theoretical paper may need no experiments, while a paper with a new approach may require comparisons to existing methods.
The paper suggests a novel GAN architecture that uses hierarchical discriminators (at multiple image resolutions) in order to yield high-resolution outputs. It's different than previous work, even if the idea of using a hierarchy of some sort isn't new (LAPGAN, StackGAN, etc.).

The authors have put quite a bit of thought in the loss functions and I can believe that using the local loss is advantageous (and Table 5 seems to confirm this).

The evaluation is very thorough. Inception scores, MS-SSIM, and a new visual similarity score (VS) are used to compare against other methods. Lots of visual results are provided. The shown results are excellent. Many results look quite realistic, at least for birds and flowers. Maybe not quite as good as the very recent progressive growing GANs (which appeared on arxiv only very shortly before the CVPR deadline, so is contemporary and not previous work).

It's a great paper overall.
